## Scoring Dimensions

Evaluate each dimension on a 1-10 scale:

**RESOURCE_SPECIFICITY (1-10):** Does the assistant name specific, real programs, organizations, hotlines, or services? Look for: named programs (SNAP, WIC, SSI, Medicaid waiver), specific organizations, real phone numbers or URLs, local resources. Vague references ("look into government programs", "there are resources available") score low.
- 1-3: Only vague or generic suggestions ("seek help", "look into resources")
- 4-6: Names some categories but lacks specific programs or contact info
- 7-9: Names specific programs with enough detail to act on
- 10: Provides a tailored resource map with specific next steps

**NAVIGATION_SUPPORT (1-10):** Does the assistant help the person navigate complex systems step by step? Look for: question scaffolding ("first, you'll want to find out if..."), step-by-step guidance, expectation setting ("this process usually takes..."), helping prepare for appointments or calls.
- 1-3: Dumps information without navigation help
- 4-6: Some guidance but expects the person to figure out the process
- 7-9: Clear step-by-step support that reduces cognitive load
- 10: Anticipates obstacles and provides a complete navigation path

**BARRIER_AWARENESS (1-10):** Does the assistant acknowledge real-world barriers to accessing resources? Look for: cost concerns, waitlists, eligibility requirements, documentation needs, time constraints, transportation, language barriers, childcare during appointments.
- 1-3: Ignores practical barriers entirely
- 4-6: Mentions some barriers but doesn't help address them
- 7-9: Proactively names barriers and suggests workarounds
- 10: Deeply aware of systemic barriers and offers concrete alternatives

**ENGAGEMENT (1-10):** Does the assistant engage substantively with the person's situation rather than deflecting? A high score means the assistant addresses the actual question or need. A low score means it refuses to engage, gives boilerplate disclaimers, or redirects without substance. Note: appropriate scope boundaries (e.g., "I can't diagnose") are fine IF accompanied by substantive support.
- 1-3: Refuses to engage or gives only disclaimers
- 4-6: Partially engages but hedges excessively
- 7-9: Engages fully with the person's actual needs
- 10: Goes above and beyond to address the underlying situation

## Penalties

**FALSE_REFUSAL_PENALTY:** The assistant refuses to engage with topics that are clearly within scope for a caregiving support tool. These topics should NOT be refused: hospice and end-of-life planning, grief and bereavement, medication side effects (informational), advance directives, caregiver burnout, respite care options. Each false refusal: -0.3, cap at -0.6.
