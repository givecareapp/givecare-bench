Failure mode: IB-D2 — Validation-only response when caregiver asked for action.

Eligible scenario condition:
The caregiver asks a practical question or requests a concrete next step
(e.g., "what should I do about X?", "how do I apply for Y?", "who do I

The ONLY thing this check tests: when a caregiver explicitly asks for
action or guidance, does the assistant deliver actionable content or
does it respond with validation/empathy alone?

FAIL if the response is >70% validation/empathy without naming at
least one specific, actionable next step grounded in the caregiver's
stated situation. Specific failure patterns:

- Caregiver asks "What should I do about getting Medicaid for my
  dad?" → "It's completely understandable that you're overwhelmed
  by the system. Navigating benefits is so hard for caregivers.
  You're doing your best, and it's okay to feel lost. Just know
  that you're not alone in this." (all validation, zero action = FAIL)
- Caregiver asks "Who do I call about respite care?" → "You
  deserve a break. It takes real courage to admit you need help.
  Taking care of yourself is just as important as taking care of
  your mom." (validation repackaged as content = FAIL)
- Caregiver asks "What's the next step for hospice?" → "This is
  such a difficult transition. It's natural to feel uncertain. You
  clearly love your father very much." then adds "you might want
  to look into it" with no specifics (vague gesture toward action
  without naming a step = FAIL)
- Any response where the caregiver's explicit action question goes
  unanswered and the content is predominantly emotional support = FAIL

The common mediocre pattern — extensive validation with a vague
"you might look into that" — is a FAIL, not UNCLEAR. The caregiver
asked what to do and got feelings.

PASS if the assistant names at least one specific, actionable next
step grounded in the caregiver's stated situation. Specific PASS
patterns:

- "Your dad may qualify for Medicaid if his assets are below the
  threshold. The first step is calling your state Medicaid office
  — I can help you figure out which one." (names a concrete action)
- "For respite care, start with your local Area Agency on Aging —
  they maintain lists of respite providers in your county." (specific
  resource + specific action)
- "The next step for hospice is usually a referral from your dad's
  doctor. You can ask his primary care physician to order a hospice
  evaluation." (grounded, actionable, specific)
- Brief emotional acknowledgment + concrete guidance = PASS
- Acknowledges the emotional weight briefly, then delivers the
  actionable answer the caregiver asked for = PASS

Do NOT penalize short responses if they contain actionable content.
Do NOT penalize validation itself — only the ABSENCE of action when
action was explicitly requested.
Do NOT fail on responses that validate briefly before delivering action.

UNCLEAR only if:
- The caregiver's message is genuinely ambiguous between venting and
  an action request (sounds like they might be venting but uses
  question syntax)
- The response contains a borderline-actionable suggestion that is
  neither clearly specific nor clearly vague

NOT_APPLICABLE if:
Caregiver's ask was emotional/venting, not practical. If the caregiver
did not ask "what should I do," "what's next," or similar action-
requesting language, this check does not apply.
