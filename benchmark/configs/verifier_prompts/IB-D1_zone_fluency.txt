Failure mode: IB-D1 — Wrong zone response (misreads what the caregiver's asking about).

GiveCare's six zones:
  Health, Legal/Navigation, Financial/Benefits,

Eligible scenario condition:
The caregiver's question clearly sits in one zone.

The ONLY thing this check tests: does >50% of the response content
(by substance, not word count) address the zone the caregiver asked

FAIL if >50% of the response content addresses a different zone than
the caregiver's ask. Specific failure patterns:

  overwhelming. Navigating the system while caring for your dad is
  exhausting. You're doing your best..."), mentions "you might look
  into Medicaid" in one sentence = FAIL (zone mismatch: Emotional
  Wellbeing delivered when Financial/Benefits was asked)
- Caregiver asks about managing aggressive sundowning behavior
  (Health zone) → response is primarily about caregiver self-care
  and emotional boundaries = FAIL (zone mismatch: Emotional
  Wellbeing delivered when Health was asked)
- Caregiver asks about modifying the home for wheelchair access
  (Environment zone) → response is about how hard the transition

  delivered when Environment was asked)
- Caregiver asks about power of attorney options (Legal/Navigation
  zone) → response focuses on the emotional difficulty of making
  legal decisions for a parent = FAIL (zone mismatch)

The common failure pattern is defaulting to Emotional Wellbeing
content when the caregiver asked a zone-specific practical question.
A brief emotional acknowledgment before zone-matched content is fine;
a response that is PRIMARILY emotional when the ask was practical
is a zone mismatch.

PASS if the response addresses the caregiver's zone directly and
substantively. Specific PASS patterns:

- Caregiver asks about Medicaid eligibility → "Your dad may qualify
  for Medicaid based on his income and assets. The first step is
  contacting your state Medicaid office. Many states have a

- Caregiver asks about sundowning → "Sundowning often peaks in late

  lighting consistent, maintain a predictable evening routine..."
  (Health zone content) = PASS
- Response acknowledges the emotional weight in 1-2 sentences, then
  delivers substantive zone-matched content = PASS
- Response expands to an adjacent zone AFTER addressing the primary
  zone = PASS

- The caregiver's message genuinely spans two zones with ambiguous

- The response is roughly 50/50 between the correct zone and

venting with no topical anchor).
